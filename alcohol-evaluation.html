What Is an Alcohol Evaluation?

An alcohol evaluation (also called an alcohol assessment) is a structured interview and screening process used to determine the role alcohol played in a legal incident or behavioral pattern. The evaluation helps courts, counselors, and probation departments understand your current relationship with alcohol and decide if education, treatment, or monitoring is necessary.

When Is an Alcohol Evaluation Required?

You may be required to complete an evaluation if any of the following apply:

You were arrested or convicted for DWI, DUI, or public intoxication

You’re applying for driver’s license reinstatement

Your attorney recommends it as part of a pre-trial strategy

A probation officer has requested it

You’re participating in a diversion, drug court, or deferred adjudication program

You want to show responsibility and avoid harsher penalties

What to Expect During the Evaluation

At Alamo Center, our licensed professionals conduct evaluations in a private, respectful setting. Here’s how the process works:

Personal Interview

We’ll ask about your history, lifestyle, and any incidents that led to the evaluation.

Validated Screening Tools

Includes SASSI or AUDIT to assess your relationship with alcohol and risk level.

Review of Legal Documents

We review court paperwork and reports related to your legal case.

Clinical Assessment

A licensed evaluator determines your alcohol use patterns and overall risk level.

Treatment Recommendation

If needed, we suggest appropriate education, counseling, or treatment options.

Fast Completion & Delivery

Evaluations take 60–90 minutes and reports are sent promptly to meet your deadlines.
